Estate agencies

Catch every lead, even from the show house

In South African property, the buyer who phones about a listing will phone the next agent if you do not answer. Your agents are rarely at a desk, they are at viewings, on the road or between appointments, so calls slip through and listings go cold.

Ring Rang rings agents on their own phones with the agency number, routes each enquiry to the right person or branch, and captures after-hours voicemail as a transcript, so a 7pm enquiry is followed up first thing.

  • Calls follow agents to their mobiles, with a private personal number kept separate
  • Route buyers by area or branch so the right agent picks up
  • After-hours and weekend enquiries captured and transcribed, never lost

Diners still phone to book, ask about specials or place a collection order, and they expect an answer. When the floor is full, the phone rings out, the table goes to a walk-in, and the booking goes to the restaurant down the road.

Ring Rang answers with a professional greeting, sends callers to the right line, and takes after-hours and overflow calls as messages or voicemail. Your hosts deal with guests in front of them while no booking is missed behind them.

Professional services

Sound like an established firm from day one

For law, accounting and medical practices, the first phone call sets the tone. Clients judge you on how the phone is answered, and a missed or fumbled call costs trust before you have said a word. Small practices often sound smaller than they are.

Ring Rang gives you a polished greeting, call groups so the right person answers, and call recording for accuracy and compliance. A solo practitioner sounds like a full firm, and a growing practice keeps that standard as it scales.
